# Using Mathematica at Home

Since IU has a site license for Mathematica, you can install it for free on your home computer in addition to using it on the lab computers.
If you prefer not to install it on your own device, it is also available on the research desktop, which you can access through a web browser

## Installing on Laptop/Desktop

To download and install Mathematica on your personal computer:

1. Navigate to [https://account.wolfram.com/products ](https://account.wolfram.com/products).
2. Do NOT create a new account; instead, enter your IU email address and click *Continue*.
    - You may be asked to sign into your IU account
3. Once logged in, click on *Wolfram Mathematica* (you will likely need to do this on two screens)
4. Download the desktop app; you can choose whether to include the documentation (larger installation) or rely on the web documentation (smaller installation)
5. The first time you open Mathematica after installation, you will be prompted to sign in to activate your product. Use the same IU username as above and confirm activation of the Mathematica site license.

## Using Research Desktop

If you'd like to avoid installing Mathematica on your personal computer, you can also use the web-based ThinLinc remote desktop client to connect to the Research Desktop (RED) environment hosted by IU.

1. Request an account on Quartz by following the instructions [here](https://servicenow.iu.edu/kb?id=kb_article_view&sysparm_article=KB0022656#research)
2. Once your account has been approved, navigate to [https://red.uits.iu.edu](https://red.uits.iu.edu) and login with your usual IU username and password
3. From the remote Linux environment in your web browser, click the top left *Applications > Analytics* menu and select Mathematica.

In order retrieve the Mathematica notebooks we will be working on in class, you can open the Firefox web browser and retrieve them in the usual way.
